How to Play Naval Battle Online game? 

Introduction

Naval Battle Online is a war-themed board game for two players in which the opponents try to guess the location of their opponent's warships and sink them. A paper and pencil version of the game dates back to World War I, but most people are familiar with the game through the plastic board game that was first marketed by the Milton Bradley Company in 1967. Since then, the game has spawned various video games and smartphone app variations.
You are on a digital version of the same game, designed by Akshay Godara.

The gameplay is straightforward.
Each player places ships on a plastic grid containing vertical and horizontal space coordinates.
Players take turns calling out row and column coordinates on the other player's grid in an attempt to identify a square that contains a ship.

Setting up the Game

Digital version is already set. 
You just have to place your ships by dragging them.
And booyah! you are ready to rock!

Basic Gameplay

Players take turns firing shots (by calling out a grid coordinate) to attempt to hit the opponent's enemy ships.

 

On your turn, simply click on your target grid. computer will automatically check that coordinate on opponent's ocean grid and the hit will remain white, if there is no ship there, or will turn red if you have correctly guessed a space that is occupied by a ship.

 

When it is your opponent's turn to fire shots at you, each time one of your ships receives a hit, the computer will put a red signal into the hole on. When one of your ships has every slot filled with red, the computer will automatically announce that your ship has been killed. In classic play, the phrase is "You sunk my battleship!"

The first player to sink all five of their opponent's ships wins the game.
